# Article Guidelines

Guidelines for writing articles in the mechanistic validity documentation. Adapted from the learn-mech-interp curriculum guidelines.

---

## What Makes an Article

### Articles are about concepts, not papers

An article should cover a **technique**, **concept**, or **idea** that stands on its own as a unit of understanding. Articles are *not* summaries of individual papers.

**Good article topics:**
- A technique: "Activation Patching," "Direct Logit Attribution," "Sparse Autoencoders"
- A concept: "Superposition," "The Linear Representation Hypothesis," "Induction Heads"
- A class of methods: "Probing Classifiers," "Steering Methods"

**Not article topics:**
- A paper: "Towards Monosemanticity"
- A model: "Interpreting GPT-2"
- A dataset or benchmark
- A case study
- A specific experimental finding

---

## Tone

- **Second-person inclusive**: "We can decompose..." not "The output can be decomposed..."
- **Direct and confident**: State claims clearly. Hedge explicitly about *why*, not vaguely.
- **Intellectually honest**: Acknowledge limitations and open questions.
- Academic but accessible. Graduate student who has the math background but is new to the topic.
- No hype language ("groundbreaking," "revolutionary," "game-changing").
- No LLM-isms ("delve into," "crucial," "it's important to note that").

---

## Structure

- **Opening**: Start with motivation. Ground in something concrete before abstractions.
- **Sections**: Clear H2 headers. Each section makes one main point. 150-400 words.
- **Definitions**: Use blockquotes for key definitions.
- **Math**: Present with context. Build up through simpler intermediate steps.
- **Cross-references**: Link to other pages where relevant.
- **Closing**: Brief "Looking Ahead" connecting to next topic(s). No summaries.

---

## Content

### Include
- Conceptual understanding (the *why*, not just the *how*)
- Concrete examples before generalizing
- Mathematical grounding motivated by intuition
- Limitations and caveats
- Original sources with proper citations

### Exclude
- Implementation details / code tutorials
- Exhaustive literature reviews
- Step-by-step reproduction guides
- Speculation beyond evidence

---

## Pedagogical Approach

- Build mental models the reader can reason with
- Concrete before abstract
- Progressive complexity (toy model before full model)
- Connect backward and forward to other concepts

---

## Formatting

- **Bold** for key terms on first introduction
- *Italics* for softer emphasis and contrasts
- Bullets for unordered items, numbers only when sequence matters
- All figures credited in captions with source
